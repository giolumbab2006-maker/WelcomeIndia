Red Fort

Outstanding Universal Value

The planning and design of the Red Fort represents a culmination of architectural development initiated in 1526 AD. It reached a splendid refinement under Shah Jahan, creating a unique fusion of Islamic, Persian, Timurid, and Hindu traditions.

Criterion (ii) & (iii)

The Red Fort is a masterpiece of innovative planning. Its architectural style and garden designs strongly influenced later structures across Rajasthan, Delhi, and Agra. It reflects the final flourishing of Mughal architecture enlivened with imported ideas and local craftsmanship.

Criterion (vi)

A powerful symbol of Indian history, the Red Fort witnessed the shift from Mughal rule to British occupation. It is the iconic site where Indian Independence was first celebrated and continues to be the heart of national celebrations today.

Integrity & Authenticity

The complex is a layered expression of Mughal and British history. While the river has changed to a road and a railway now divides the Salimgarh Fort, the site remains inextricably linked to India's regional identity and geo-cultural history.

Protection & Management

Declared a monument of national importance under the Ancient Monument and Archaeological Sites and Remains Act, 1959, the fort is managed by the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI). Efforts continue to ensure that visitor impact is managed to prevent the decay of this stable yet delicate heritage site.
