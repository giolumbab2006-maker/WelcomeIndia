Greeting - The Namaste

The Art of Namaste

Namaste is more than just a greeting; it is an expression of goodwill and deep-rooted respect. While different cultures use handshakes or bowing, Namaste remains the customary greeting of the Indian subcontinent, Southeast Asia, and the global Indian Diaspora.

"I Bow to the Divine in You"

Spoken with a slight bow and palms pressed together near the chest, the literal meaning of Namaste is an attempt to unite the spiritual oneness between two people. It has existed since ancient Vedic times as a gesture of worship and welcome.

The Science of No-Contact

Recently, Namaste grabbed global attention as a safe, no-contact greeting. By avoiding physical touch, it significantly reduces the spread of infections—a practice supported by the WHO as a key pillar of hygiene and safety in communities.

The Tradition of Hygiene

Hands are frequent carriers of microorganisms. By choosing the Namaste Pose over a handshake, we protect ourselves and others from pathogens transferred via touch. It is a timeless tradition that balances spiritual connection with modern scientific wisdom.

"Hygiene is the first pillar of safety." - World Health Organization
