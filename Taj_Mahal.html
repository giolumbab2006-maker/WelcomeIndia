Taj Mahal

Outstanding Universal Value

The Taj Mahal is located on the right bank of the Yamuna River in a vast Mughal garden that encompasses nearly 17 hectares. Built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, it is the greatest architectural achievement in the whole range of Indo-Islamic architecture.

Architectural Marvel

Construction started in 1632 AD and was completed in 1648 AD. The main architect, Ustad-Ahmad Lahori, combined rhythmic solids and voids, arches, and domes to create a masterpiece that changes tints with the blue sky and sun.

Unique Innovation

Unlike other tombs, it is placed at one end of the garden rather than the center, adding depth to the view. It features four free-standing minarets that give the building a three-dimensional effect never seen before in its time.

Integrity & Authenticity

The Taj Mahal complex is protected by the Archaeological Survey of India. To prevent decay from pollution, a 10,400 sq km area called the Taj Trapezium Zone (TTZ) was established to ban coal and coke use in nearby industries.
