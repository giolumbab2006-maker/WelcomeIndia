Symbols - Fasting

The Tradition of Fasting

In Hinduism, fasting is not an obligation but a moral and spiritual act. It serves to purify the body and mind, helping individuals acquire divine grace through self-discipline and devotion.

Forms & Varieties

Fasting varies based on personal and community beliefs. It might involve skipping one meal or simply replacing certain foods. For instance, vegetarians might swap wheat and rice for potatoes or enjoy special treats like Modaks (sweet coconut dumplings) during Ganesh worship.

Sacred Periods

The most common fast is Ekadashi, occurring twice a month. Other significant times include Mondays and Saturdays during July and August, or the Great Night of Shiva (Mahashivratri) at the start of the year.

Mahatma Gandhi & Fasting

Mahatma Gandhi elevated fasting from a religious conviction to a powerful tool for social change. He used hunger strikes as a method of Satyagraha (non-violent resistance) to protest violence and exert political pressure during India’s struggle for independence.

"Fasting is a potent weapon in the armoury of Satyagraha." - Mahatma Gandhi
